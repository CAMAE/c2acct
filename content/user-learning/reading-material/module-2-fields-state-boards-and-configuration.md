# Module 2: Fields of Study, State Boards, and Configuration Discipline

## Position

This module is part of an internal professional-learning package. It is designed to be CPE-ready in architecture and accreditation-aligned in structure, but it does not claim approved CPE credit.

## Field of Study Focus

Regulatory Ethics / Management Services

## Module Purpose

Explains why field tagging and state-board variability must be modeled explicitly if the learning layer is to remain honest and exportable.

## Learning Objectives

- Connect module design to official fields of study.
- Explain how state-board variability affects reporting and course claims.
- Identify evidence fields needed for jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction configuration.

## Key Topics

### New York concentration model
- Principle: New York distinguishes between concentrated subject-area reporting and mixed-hour reporting.
- Operational implication: Design learner records so concentrated and mixed tracks can be reported separately.
- Required evidence artifact: A transcript-style export showing hours, field, and reporting path per module.
- Primary failure risk: Course history cannot be reorganized into the reporting structure the jurisdiction expects.
- Why it matters: State models can be operationally different even when the learning content overlaps.
- Source: SRC04 | NYSED Mandatory Continuing Education Q&A | https://www.op.nysed.gov/professions/certified-public-accountants/mce-questions-answers

### California board-defined requirements
- Principle: California demonstrates that continuing-education compliance is board-defined rather than vendor-defined.
- Operational implication: Keep board-facing assumptions outside marketing language and inside explicit compliance configuration.
- Required evidence artifact: A state-rule reference attached to module metadata rather than implied in the course title.
- Primary failure risk: The platform presents a course as accepted when the board has not accepted that claim.
- Why it matters: Board authority, not vendor preference, determines what can be represented to licensees.
- Source: SRC05 | California Board CE Requirements | https://www.dca.ca.gov/cba/licensees/ce-requirements.shtml

### Virginia documentation posture
- Principle: Virginia board guidance emphasizes documentation, carryforward treatment, and audit-facing readiness.
- Operational implication: Store records so carryforward, annual completion, and supporting evidence can be reconstructed later.
- Required evidence artifact: Learner-level records preserving timestamps, completed content, and carryforward-relevant details.
- Primary failure risk: A learner may have creditable work but no supportable documentation trail.
- Why it matters: Recordkeeping needs are part of competence maintenance, not just back-office work.
- Source: SRC06 | Virginia Board CPE Handout | https://boa.virginia.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/CPE-Informational-Handout.pdf

### Illinois renewal documentation
- Principle: Illinois illustrates that renewal documentation can require category and sponsor information beyond raw completion counts.
- Operational implication: Retain source titles, provider posture, and category metadata alongside scores.
- Required evidence artifact: A source-coded completion record that can be translated into jurisdictional renewal support.
- Primary failure risk: Completion data exists but lacks the metadata required for renewal support.
- Why it matters: If metadata is missing, otherwise useful learning records become non-portable.
- Source: SRC07 | Illinois CPA CPE FAQs | https://idfpr.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/idfpr/faq/dpr/cpa-cpe-faqs-6-28-18.pdf

### Texas and Florida category logic
- Principle: Texas and Florida show that technical classification, ethics treatment, and provider rules require explicit tracking.
- Operational implication: Separate core field tagging, ethics flags, and provider-status assumptions in the data model.
- Required evidence artifact: A module profile containing field of study, ethics relevance, and jurisdiction notes.
- Primary failure risk: Technical, behavioral, or ethics content is reported inaccurately.
- Why it matters: Category treatment affects how a course can be counted and whether a learner can rely on it.
- Source: SRC08 | Texas CPE Guidelines | https://www.tsbpa.texas.gov/php/fpl/options/CPEGuidelines.html
