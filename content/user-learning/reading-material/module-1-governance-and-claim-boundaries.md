# Module 1: Governance, Claim Boundaries, and CPE-Ready Positioning

## Position

This module is part of an internal professional-learning package. It is designed to be CPE-ready in architecture and accreditation-aligned in structure, but it does not claim approved CPE credit.

## Field of Study Focus

Regulatory Ethics

## Module Purpose

Defines what the platform can claim, what evidence it needs, and why truth-in-claims is part of the learning architecture.

## Learning Objectives

- Distinguish internal professional learning from approved CPE sponsorship.
- Explain why claim boundaries are governance controls.
- Identify the minimum evidence posture for CPE-ready architecture.

## Key Topics

### Truthful claim posture
- Principle: AAE/C2Acct may be described as internal professional learning with CPE-ready architecture, but not as an approved CPE sponsor.
- Operational implication: State the platform posture as internal professional learning with evidence and configuration seams.
- Required evidence artifact: A documented claim-boundary statement paired with source-mapped learning records.
- Primary failure risk: Misrepresentation of sponsor authority and erosion of institutional trust.
- Why it matters: Professional-learning systems lose credibility when claims exceed documented authority.
- Source: SRC01 | NASBA / AICPA / QAS CPE Standards | https://www.nasbaregistry.org/sponsor/site/docs/CPEStandards.pdf

### Sponsor-level controls
- Principle: CPE-oriented learning requires documented instructional standards, completion evidence, and retention discipline.
- Operational implication: Design completion, scoring, and evidence storage before making stronger external claims.
- Required evidence artifact: A retained completion record with score, timestamp, source map, and assessment version.
- Primary failure risk: A learner can complete content without leaving audit-grade proof.
- Why it matters: Standards-based learning depends on records that can be defended after the event.
- Source: SRC01 | NASBA / AICPA / QAS CPE Standards | https://www.nasbaregistry.org/sponsor/site/docs/CPEStandards.pdf

### Official taxonomy
- Principle: Fields-of-study labels should follow official terminology rather than ad hoc naming.
- Operational implication: Map modules to NASBA-aligned fields before presenting them as professionally structured learning.
- Required evidence artifact: A field-of-study matrix tied to module objectives and assessment records.
- Primary failure risk: Learning topics become difficult to classify, export, or govern across jurisdictions.
- Why it matters: Official taxonomy is the cleanest path to serious reporting and later state configuration.
- Source: SRC02 | NASBA Fields of Study | https://www.nasbaregistry.org/resources/fields-of-study

### Jurisdiction sensitivity
- Principle: Provider acceptance and subject treatment can vary by state board and by reporting path.
- Operational implication: Treat state recognition as a configurable layer instead of a universal system claim.
- Required evidence artifact: State-specific metadata showing jurisdiction, field, and reporting assumptions.
- Primary failure risk: A one-size-fits-all claim can contradict a board-specific acceptance rule.
- Why it matters: State models can differ materially even when content overlaps.
- Source: SRC04 | NYSED Mandatory Continuing Education Q&A | https://www.op.nysed.gov/professions/certified-public-accountants/mce-questions-answers

### Completion evidence
- Principle: Completion evidence is part of the learning product, not an administrative afterthought.
- Operational implication: Retain evidence artifacts at learner, module, quiz, and final-assessment levels.
- Required evidence artifact: An exportable record linking learner, module outcome, score, and cited source basis.
- Primary failure risk: The system can report progress but cannot prove it later.
- Why it matters: If evidence is not retained with the event, later validation becomes fragile.
- Source: SRC01 | NASBA / AICPA / QAS CPE Standards | https://www.nasbaregistry.org/sponsor/site/docs/CPEStandards.pdf
