## Direct notes from the extracted text

- The framework explicitly says it is built around guidance without force and rejects manipulation, intimidation, and artificial urgency as the operating model.
- Module 0 centers repetition, probability, and emotional neutrality so operators do not overreact to single outcomes.
- Module 1 argues that pressure creates resistance while structure creates safety and movement.
- The text treats indifference, drift, and decision decay as a greater problem than overt objection.
- The framework repeatedly prioritizes emotional safety, calm confidence, timing, and pacing over more facts or more volume.
- Module 2 treats staged questioning as a pacing and ownership device rather than simple information gathering.
- Module 3 emphasizes fixed sequence, visible flow, and structure as a source of safety.
- Module 4 distinguishes urgency from fear but still uses same-day decision framing that is not appropriate for regulated professional learning.
- Module 5 emphasizes consolidation and follow-up to protect decisions from unraveling.

## Ethical carry-forward into AAE

Carry forward:
- sequence
- pacing
- progress framing
- calm authority
- reduced overwhelm
- modular reinforcement

Do not carry forward:
- decision-window pressure
- same-day close logic
- sales-style urgency framing
- any tactic designed to impair reflective judgment
