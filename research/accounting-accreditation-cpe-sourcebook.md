# Accounting Accreditation and CPE Sourcebook

Accessed: 2026-03-16

## Scope

This file separates:
- official-source facts
- university and professional-society guidance
- design recommendations for AAE/C2Acct

No source reviewed in this pass proves that AAE/C2Acct is an approved CPE sponsor or that its current learning layer is already approved for CPE credit issuance.

## Official-source facts

- NASBA states that fields of study are the taxonomy used by the National Registry of CPE Sponsors to classify continuing professional education content. Source: https://www.nasbaregistry.org/resources/fields-of-study
- The current NASBA/AICPA/QAS Statement on Standards for Continuing Professional Education requires sponsor-level controls around instructional design, documentation, attendance/completion evidence, and record retention. Source: https://www.nasbaregistry.org/sponsor/site/docs/CPEStandards.pdf
- AACSB business accreditation standards continue to center assurance of learning, curriculum management, learner progression, and continuous improvement. Source: https://www.aacsb.edu/accredited/standards/business
- New York requires either 24 hours in one recognized concentration or 40 hours in mixed subject areas, plus ethics requirements, and ties acceptable providers to NYSED, NASBA, or another state board. Sources:
  - https://www.op.nysed.gov/professions/certified-public-accountants/mce-questions-answers
  - https://www.op.nysed.gov/professions/certified-public-accountants/laws-rules-regulations/part-70
- California states continuing education requirements at the board level and distinguishes licensing compliance from vendor marketing claims. Source: https://www.dca.ca.gov/cba/licensees/ce-requirements.shtml
- Virginia publishes board-level CPE guidance that emphasizes subject-area fit, carryforward limits, and documentation. Source: https://boa.virginia.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/CPE-Informational-Handout.pdf
- Illinois requires 120 hours each three-year renewal period, including ethics, and publishes CPA-specific CPE FAQs. Sources:
  - https://idfpr.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/idfpr/faq/dpr/cpa-cpe-faqs-6-28-18.pdf
  - https://idfpr.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/idfpr/renewals/apply/forms/f1574-cpa-conted.pdf
- Texas treats CPE as a documented reporting obligation, limits some non-technical content, and requires retained evidence. Sources:
  - https://www.tsbpa.texas.gov/php/fpl/options/CPEGuidelines.html
  - https://www.tsbpa.texas.gov/info/submitCPE.html
  - https://www.tsbpa.texas.gov/pdffiles/br/br202505.pdf
- Florida publishes both CPE guidelines and annual audit expectations, including board-approved ethics treatment. Sources:
  - https://www2.myfloridalicense.com/certified-public-accounting/licensure/continuing-professional-education/
  - https://www2.myfloridalicense.com/cpa/documents/CPEGuidelines.pdf
  - https://www2.myfloridalicense.com/certified-public-accounting/annual-cpe-audit/

## University and professional-society guidance

- Rutgers Business School publishes explicit accounting learning goals, including communication, quantitative skill, IT skill, ethics, and critical thinking. Source: https://myrbs.business.rutgers.edu/undergraduate-newark/accounting-minor
- Rutgers also runs stackable graduate-level concentrations in continuous auditing and IT audit, which is directly relevant to modular professional learning design. Sources:
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable/continuous-auditing
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable/information-technology-audit
- Indiana Kelley publishes accounting program competencies and student learning outcomes for its MS in Accounting and Data Analytics. Source: https://kelley.iu.edu/about/directory/offices/instructional-consulting/assessment/accounting.html
- Indiana Kelley also frames accounting upskilling through executive education, applied AI, and experiential learning rather than only degree coursework. Sources:
  - https://kelley.iu.edu/programs/ms-accounting/index.html
  - https://kelley.iu.edu/executive-education/professional-development/leading-with-ai/ai-accounting/index.html
- The IIA, IMA, and ISACA all require ongoing continuing education plus auditability or annual attestation to keep credentials active. Sources:
  - https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/cia/cpe-requirements/
  - https://www.theiia.org/en/standards/2024-standards/future-of-the-ippf-evolution/ethics-and-professionalism/
  - https://www.imanet.org/en/IMA-Certifications/CMA-Certification/Maintain
  - https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cgeit/maintain-cgeit-certification

## Design recommendations for AAE/C2Acct

- Truthful posture now:
  - internal professional learning
  - accreditation-aligned educational framework
  - CPE-ready architecture
  - state-configurable compliance path
- Unsupported posture now:
  - not an approved CPE sponsor
  - not an approved CPE issuer
  - not state-board accepted credit without sponsor and jurisdiction validation
- The repo should keep:
  - source maps for every learning object
  - completion evidence
  - assessment versioning
  - learner-level audit history
  - state-board configuration seams, not universal claims
- If CPE pursuit becomes real later, the next non-repo dependency is governance:
  - sponsor authority
  - jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction rule mapping
  - attendance/completion evidence policy
  - retention policy
  - complaints/contact path
