# Firm Module Research Sourcebook

Accessed: 2026-03-16

## Official-source facts relevant to firm modules

- GAO Green Book provides a formal internal-control framework with operations, reporting, and compliance objectives plus preventive and detective control guidance. Sources:
  - https://www.gao.gov/greenbook
  - https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-107721
- IRS recordkeeping guidance is explicit that businesses need records to prepare financial statements, support tax positions, and monitor operations. Sources:
  - https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/recordkeeping
  - https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/what-kind-of-records-should-i-keep
  - https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/why-should-i-keep-records
- NIST CSF 2.0 helps firms integrate governance, supply-chain risk, and cybersecurity management into their operating model. Source: https://www.nist.gov/publications/nist-cybersecurity-framework-csf-20
- SEC cybersecurity disclosure guidance matters for firms that are public or serve public-company workflows. Sources:
  - https://www.sec.gov/corpfin/secg-cybersecurity
  - https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2023-139
- NASBA fields and CPE standards support ongoing internal professional learning in accounting-adjacent operations. Sources:
  - https://www.nasbaregistry.org/resources/fields-of-study
  - https://www.nasbaregistry.org/sponsor/site/docs/CPEStandards.pdf

## Recommended firm module themes

1. Internal control and documentation discipline
2. Recordkeeping and evidence retention
3. Workflow fit between firm practice and product stack
4. Risk governance and incident readiness
5. Professional-learning upkeep for accounting-facing staff

## Design recommendations for AAE

- Firm modules should stay self-owned and not be mislabeled as third-party assurance.
- Modules should generate evidence useful for internal maturity, not audit opinion language.
- Insight unlocks should focus on:
  - documentation quality
  - operating discipline
  - product-process alignment
  - learning completion and recency
- Strongest initial field mapping:
  - Management Services
  - Auditing
  - Information Technology
  - Regulatory Ethics
