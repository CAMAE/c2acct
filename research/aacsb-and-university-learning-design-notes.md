# AACSB and University Learning Design Notes

Accessed: 2026-03-16

## Official and university guidance

- AACSB business accreditation standards anchor learning design around learner success, assurance of learning, curriculum management, and continuous improvement. Source: https://www.aacsb.edu/accredited/standards/business
- Rutgers publishes explicit undergraduate accounting learning goals that include communication, business knowledge, quantitative skill, critical thinking, information technology, ethics, and multicultural awareness. Source: https://myrbs.business.rutgers.edu/undergraduate-newark/accounting-minor
- Rutgers also publishes stackable graduate-level certificate architecture that lets learners accumulate smaller units toward larger credentials. Source: https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable
- Rutgers continuous auditing and IT audit concentrations show that serious accounting learning can be modular, technical, and practice-oriented without becoming lightweight. Sources:
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable/continuous-auditing
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable/information-technology-audit
- Rutgers governmental accounting online MAcc shows that asynchronous and career-compatible delivery can still sit inside a serious accounting frame. Source: https://www.business.rutgers.edu/masters-governmental-accounting
- Indiana Kelley publishes explicit accounting competencies and student learning outcomes for its MSADA program. Source: https://kelley.iu.edu/about/directory/offices/instructional-consulting/assessment/accounting.html
- Indiana Kelley frames accounting advancement through data analytics, experiential learning, and executive education rather than only seat-time or degree prestige. Sources:
  - https://kelley.iu.edu/programs/ms-accounting/index.html
  - https://kelley.iu.edu/executive-education/professional-development/leading-with-ai/ai-accounting/index.html
  - https://kelley.iu.edu/programs/executive-education-degrees-certificates/online-certificates/business-analytics/index.html

## What this supports for AAE/C2Acct

- Modular progression is supportable when each module has explicit outcomes and evidence.
- Adult learners respond well to stackable, practice-linked structures when the content stays serious.
- Assurance-of-learning logic favors:
  - stated competencies
  - direct assessment evidence
  - periodic review
  - curriculum-to-outcome mapping
- AAE should therefore keep:
  - module objectives
  - question-level source maps
  - graded checkpoints
  - final assessment evidence
  - revision history

## What this does not support

- A claim that any current AAE module is university-accredited
- A claim that the current repo is AACSB-approved
- A claim that stackable modules automatically qualify as degree credit or CPE

## Design recommendations

- Use an assurance-of-learning model, not an edtech toy model.
- Keep module outcomes explicit and measurable.
- Keep evidence cumulative across reading, quiz, and final assessment layers.
- Treat badges as evidence markers and progression labels, not as game points.
