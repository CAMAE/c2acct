# Gamification and Engagement Sourcebook

Accessed: 2026-03-16

## Guardrail

This file is not a license to make professional education frivolous. The objective is disciplined engagement for serious adult learners.

## Source-backed signals

- NASBA/AICPA/QAS CPE Standards require instructional integrity, not entertainment-first mechanics. Source: https://www.nasbaregistry.org/sponsor/site/docs/CPEStandards.pdf
- AACSB standards prioritize learner success and evidence-based improvement over novelty. Source: https://www.aacsb.edu/accredited/standards/business
- Rutgers stackable programming shows that modularity and optional accumulation can increase participation without reducing seriousness. Source: https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable
- Rutgers continuous auditing and IT audit offerings show that narrowly scoped, career-relevant modules can remain rigorous. Sources:
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable/continuous-auditing
  - https://www.business.rutgers.edu/stackable/information-technology-audit
- Indiana Kelley uses applied projects, case work, and optional badge-like executive recognition rather than arcade-style reward loops. Source: https://kelley.iu.edu/executive-education/professional-development/leading-with-ai/ai-accounting/index.html
- The IIA ties social badging and registry visibility to credential maintenance rather than arcade mechanics. Sources:
  - https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/iap/
  - https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/certification-registry/
- The IIA OnDemand model demonstrates that flexible, self-paced, always-available learning can still be audit-grade and CPE-oriented. Sources:
  - https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/already-certified/cpe-requirements/cpe-opportunities/
  - https://www.theiia.org/en/learning/online/ondemand/iia-ondemand-subscription/
- IMA and ISACA both maintain annual renewal, ethics, and auditability expectations, reinforcing that ongoing engagement must remain evidence-based. Sources:
  - https://www.imanet.org/en/IMA-Certifications/CMA-Certification/Maintain
  - https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cgeit/maintain-cgeit-certification

## Recommended engagement patterns

- Visible progression:
  - module progress bars
  - completed/not-completed states
  - evidence-backed unlocks
- Serious recognition:
  - badges that stand for completed competencies
  - printable completion artifacts
  - profile-visible milestone records
- Reduced overwhelm:
  - 5-question pages
  - clear checkpoint boundaries
  - shorter module summaries before harder assessments
- Persistence support:
  - calm reminders
  - next-best-action prompts
  - resume-where-you-left-off behavior
- Professional motivation:
  - role relevance
  - firm/product context
  - explicit explanation of why the lesson matters operationally

## Patterns to avoid

- artificial urgency
- streak mechanics that punish working professionals
- random rewards unrelated to learning evidence
- manipulative pressure language
- fake unlocks that do not correspond to earned evidence

## Design recommendation

AAE should use `engagement through structure`, not `engagement through pressure`. The safest pattern is:
- clear objective
- short serious reading
- small quiz checkpoint
- visible completion evidence
- unlock of the next relevant layer
